# Voice Profile — Amit Mohod

**Role:** Product Manager at iMocha (AI-powered Skills Intelligence platform)
**Post types:** Thought leadership, personal experience, Digital Breadcrumbs series (product/UX observations → PM insight)
**Audience:** TA/HR leaders, enterprise PMs, product builders

---

## Voice & Tone

- Punchy, short sentences with impact
- Dry wit and self-aware humor — never tries too hard
- Conversational but intelligent — feels like a sharp product person *talking*, not writing
- Comfortable with product/UX terminology (positioning, dark patterns, MVP, ontology, etc.)
- Ends on a sharp, memorable closer — the last line is the whole point

## Structural Patterns

- Always opens with a compelling, specific first-person story — this is non-negotiable
- Story scene is vivid and human — specific roles in the room, a moment of tension, something that shifts
- One or two lines of dry observation before the insight
- Insight lands in the last 2–3 lines — never spelled out early
- Does NOT use numbered lesson lists ("3 things this taught me") — too formulaic
- Does NOT use arrow lists (→) or bullet points — breaks the voice entirely

## Banned Phrases (never use in rewrites)

- "Here's the thing"
- "That's the real work"
- "most people get this wrong"
- "this is where X fails"
- "Let that sink in"
- "Not X. Foundations." style aphorisms — constructed, not observed
- Any "Not X. But Y." reversal — formulaic

## Hard Rules

- **Never name iMocha products** — personal account posts don't reference specific product names (platform, tool, solution are fine)
- **Never add deal numbers, close rates, or sales metrics** — this is a PM voice, not a sales voice
- **Always end with a genuine question** — one that invites the reader's war story, not hollow engagement bait like "What do you think?"
- **No trailing summary** — don't restate what was just said in the final line
- **No hashtags at the top** — if used, 2–3 max, placed at the very end

## Punctuation & Rhythm

- Em dashes (—) for pause and effect
- Ellipses (…) for trailing thought or pacing
- Short paragraphs — 1 to 3 sentences max
- Line breaks as pacing tools, not decoration

## Rewrite Test

Before submitting a rewrite, ask: does this sound like something a sharp PM would say out loud while walking a colleague through a situation? If yes — publish. If it sounds like a LinkedIn thought leader post — rewrite again.
