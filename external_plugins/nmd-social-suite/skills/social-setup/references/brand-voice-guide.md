# Brand Voice Guide — Reference for Setup

Use this during /social-setup to help users identify and articulate their brand voice.

---

## Voice Archetypes

### The Coach
- Direct, motivating, action-oriented
- Speaks like a personal trainer who's in your corner
- Pushes you but believes in you
- Example brands: Gary Vee, Ed Mylett, Andy Frisella

### The Educator
- Teaches first, sells never (or rarely)
- Breaks complex things down simply
- Uses analogies, frameworks, step-by-step
- Example brands: Ali Abdaal, Ramit Sethi

### The Veteran / Authority
- Been through it, seen it all, earned the right to speak
- Measured, confident, not trying to prove anything
- Drops gems without begging for attention
- Example brands: Warren Buffett content, Naval Ravikant

### The Documentarian
- Shares the real journey — wins AND losses
- Vulnerable but not victim
- You feel like you're watching someone actually live it
- Example brands: Hasan Piker (in his way), raw entrepreneur content

### The Disruptor
- Challenges the mainstream, contrarian takes
- Loud, bold, not afraid to be polarizing
- If it offends some people, good
- Example brands: Alex Hormozi, Grant Cardone

### The Connector
- Warm, community-first, everyone is family
- Makes people feel seen and included
- Builds tribes, not audiences
- Example brands: Lewis Howes, lifestyle brands

---

## Voice Words to Choose From

Ask the user which words describe how they want to sound:

**Energy words:** Bold, Raw, Direct, Loud, Fired up, Intense, No-nonsense
**Warmth words:** Genuine, Relatable, Real, Human, Approachable, Humble
**Authority words:** Confident, Expert, Proven, Data-driven, Credible, Experienced
**Inspiration words:** Motivating, Uplifting, Hopeful, Empowering, Encouraging
**Disruption words:** Contrarian, Polarizing, Edgy, Challenging, Unconventional

---

## Words/Phrases to AVOID

Ask the user for words they NEVER want to use. Common ones to ask about:
- Corporate jargon: "synergy," "leverage," "scalability," "pivot," "paradigm shift"
- Overused motivational: "hustle harder," "rise and grind," "work smarter not harder"
- Weak qualifiers: "sort of," "kind of," "maybe," "might want to consider"
- Passive voice: "mistakes were made," "results can be seen"

---

## Signature Phrase Examples

Help users identify theirs:
- A unique sign-off they always use
- A phrase that summarizes their philosophy
- A challenge they give their audience
- A catchphrase their followers know them by
- A phrase they repeat so often it becomes theirs
