## How to Use Competitor Data in Campaigns

### Content Gap Analysis
Before creating authority content, check what competitors are NOT covering:

1. Query competitor activity for the past 4 weeks
2. Identify topics with low competitor coverage but high market relevance
3. Prioritize those topics for Recruitin's LinkedIn content
4. Use contrarian or data_story style to differentiate

### Competitive Response Strategy

| Competitor Threat Level | Response |
|------------------------|----------|
| HOOG (active + relevant) | Create counter-content within 48 hours on the same topic with Recruitin's unique angle |
| GEMIDDELD (moderate activity) | Monitor weekly, plan content that covers their gaps |
| LAAG (occasional activity) | No direct response needed, maintain standard publishing |
| MINIMAAL (inactive) | Opportunity to dominate the topic space |

### Timing Strategy
- Avoid publishing within 24 hours of a competitor's major post on the same topic
- If competitor posts on Monday, schedule Recruitin's response for Wednesday-Thursday
- During low competitor activity weeks, increase Recruitin's posting frequency

## Threat Assessment Formula

```
threat_score = (blog_posts × 3) + (linkedin_posts × 5) + (news_mentions × 2) + (vacancy_growth × 2)

Activity Level:
  ZEER ACTIEF: total_content ≥ 5 AND avg_relevance ≥ 50%
  ACTIEF: total_content ≥ 3 AND avg_relevance ≥ 30%
  MATIG ACTIEF: total_content ≥ 1 OR avg_relevance ≥ 20%
  INACTIEF: else

Threat Level:
  HOOG: ZEER ACTIEF AND avg_relevance ≥ 60%
  GEMIDDELD: ACTIEF AND avg_relevance ≥ 40%
  LAAG: MATIG ACTIEF
  MINIMAAL: INACTIEF
```
